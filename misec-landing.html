The Science

How depression changes the way we sound and speak.

Depression doesn't only affect mood — it reshapes voice and language in measurable ways.

Speech Patterns Shift

Depressed individuals often exhibit reduced pitch variation, longer pauses, lower vocal energy, and increased jitter. Our audio model extracts OpenSMILE features — pitch, MFCCs, loudness, and shimmer — to detect these micro-changes with precision.

Language Turns Inward

Written and spoken text from depressed individuals tends toward negative sentiment, hopelessness, and self-focus. Our text model captures these semantic shifts using deep contextual embeddings, spotting signals that humans often miss.

A complete multimodal detection pipeline.

Audio Analysis

A 3-layer CNN processes OpenSMILE acoustic features — pitch, jitter, MFCCs, loudness, and shimmer — to detect vocal biomarkers linked to depression severity.

Text Analysis

An Attention BiLSTM reads between the lines of language, paired with a Stacking Ensemble of ML models for robust, context-aware classification.

Multimodal Fusion

Audio and text signals are fused with configurable weights, letting the model leverage both modalities for more confident, well-rounded predictions.

Explainable AI
